1. **Flight School Administrator – “Sarah the Scheduler”**
- **Role:** Operations manager or admin at a Part 61 or Part 141 flight school
- **Age:** 30–50
- **Goals:**
    - Streamline scheduling for instructors, students, aircraft, and simulators
    - Reduce no-shows and double-bookings
    - Ensure compliance with FAA regulations
- **Pain Points:**
    - Managing multiple calendars and paper forms
    - Difficulty communicating last-minute changes to students and instructors
    - Limited visibility into aircraft downtime and maintenance schedules
- **Key Features They Value:**
    - Centralized scheduling dashboard
    - Automated reminders and notifications
    - Real-time availability updates

### 2. **Flight Instructor – “Mike the CFI”**

- **Role:** Certified Flight Instructor working full-time or part-time
- **Age:** 24–40
- **Goals:**
    - Maximize paid instruction time
    - Coordinate schedules with multiple students easily
    - Track student progress and lesson plans
- **Pain Points:**
    - Juggling multiple apps or spreadsheets for scheduling and student progress
    - Last-minute cancellations or weather delays affecting pay
- **Key Features They Value:**
    - Mobile-friendly scheduling
    - Quick access to student profiles and training plans
    - Notifications about schedule changes or aircraft status

### 3. **Student Pilot – “Alyssa the Aspiring Aviator”**
- **Role:** Enrolled student pilot training for Private or Commercial certificate
- **Age:** 17–35
- **Goals:**
    - Book lessons and aircraft without back-and-forth calls
    - Track training milestones and currency
    - Stay informed about cancellations and updates
- **Pain Points:**
    - Confusion over aircraft or instructor availability
    - Missed notifications or outdated schedules
- **Key Features They Value:**
    - Easy online booking
    - Lesson reminders and push notifications
    - Progress tracker tied to their training syllabus
