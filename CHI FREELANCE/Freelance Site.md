# 6. Create your own freelance website

Your site should be simple and direct.

## Site structure

### Homepage

Headline:

> Websites for Chicago small businesses that need more leads, bookings, and customers.

Subheadline:

> I design and build clean, mobile-friendly websites for restaurants, local service businesses, wellness brands, and entrepreneurs.

CTA:

> Book a free website review

Sections:

- What you do
- Who you help
- Packages
- Portfolio
- Why work with you
- Testimonials or sample audits
- Contact form

---

### Services page

Break down your packages:

- Website Refresh
- Full Small Business Website
- Landing Page
- Monthly Care Plan

Make the outcomes very clear.

Bad:

> I offer web development and design services.

Better:

> I help local businesses turn outdated websites into modern, mobile-friendly sales tools that make it easier for customers to call, book, order, or inquire.

---

### Portfolio page

Use 3–5 case studies.

Even if they are sample projects, label them honestly as concept builds.

---

### About page

You should lean into:

- Chicago-based
- Marketing background
- Purdue degree
- Experience with go-to-market, email, positioning, and client deliverables
- Ability to think beyond visuals

A strong angle:

> I do not just build websites that look good. I build websites around what your customers need to see before they call, book, or buy.

---

### Contact page

Use a simple form:

- Name
- Business name
- Website URL
- What do you need help with?
- Budget range
- Timeline

Also include:

> Not sure what you need? Send me your current website and I’ll send back a quick review.

That “free review” offer is your lead magnet.
