## A. Neighborhood chambers

Chicago has many neighborhood chambers, including Lakeview, Lincoln Park, Ravenswood, Hyde Park, Edgewater, Northcenter, Uptown, West Town, Rogers Park, Jefferson Park, and others listed through chamber directories. The West Town Chamber, for example, describes itself as a nonprofit with hundreds of small business members and maintains a business directory/community calendar.

Use chambers in two ways:

1. **Find businesses to prospect**
2. **Attend networking events**

Do not join every chamber immediately. Start by using public directories.

### Neighborhoods to prioritize

Given your likely location flexibility, I’d look at:

- Lakeview
- Lincoln Park
- Wicker Park / West Town
- Logan Square
- Ravenswood
- Andersonville / Edgewater
- River North
- West Loop
- South Loop
- Hyde Park

These neighborhoods have lots of small businesses that need websites, booking pages, SEO, and marketing help.

## B. Local business directories

Some chamber directories include categories like marketing, professional services, restaurants, healthcare, real estate, salons, and web design/development. The Lincoln Park Chamber directory, for example, has a wide range of categories including restaurants, healthcare, salons, real estate, marketing/PR, and web design/development. Ravenswood’s directory also lists categories such as cafés, computer services, marketing services, nonprofits, restaurants, retail, salons, and professional services.

Use these directories to build a prospect list.

### Prospecting workflow

For each business:

1. Open their website.
2. Check mobile experience.
3. Check if the site has a clear CTA.
4. Check page speed roughly.
5. Check if hours/contact/location are easy to find.
6. Check if the site looks outdated.
7. Check if the business has recent Google reviews.
8. Send a personalized audit.

You are looking for businesses that are active but underperforming online.

## C. Meetups and tech communities

Chicago has active tech and startup communities. Meetup lists web development events near Chicago, including groups like Digital Delivery Chicago and Stripe Chicago events. Digital Delivery Chicago hosts software/product/UX-related talks in the Loop and lists recurring 2026 meetup dates. There are also AI and builder communities, such as AI Tinkerers Chicago, which focuses on hands-on AI builders and technical networking.

Use meetups differently depending on the audience:

|Event type|Goal|
|---|---|
|Small business events|Find clients|
|Tech meetups|Find collaborators/referrals|
|Startup events|Find early-stage founders|
|Marketing events|Find agency overflow work|
|Chamber events|Build local trust|

At tech meetups, do not pitch “I make websites.” Instead say:

> I help small businesses and early-stage founders turn their website into something that actually converts. I also have a marketing background, so I can help with positioning and launch strategy.

That is more memorable.

# 8. Build a local outreach system

You need a repeatable lead engine.

## Weekly prospecting target

For your first 90 days:

- Add 50 businesses/week to your lead list
- Send 25 personalized emails/week
- Send 10 Instagram/LinkedIn messages/week
- Attend 1 local event/week
- Do 3 free mini-audits/week
- Ask for 5 referrals/week

That sounds like a lot, but it is manageable if you batch it.
